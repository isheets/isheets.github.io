a disruptive argument that shines light on the downsides of driving.

you know when you're driving and all you can think is

"damn this sucks."

well you're not alone! everybody hates driving and now we know why!

IT SUCKS.

but why specifically?

well there's a variety of different reasons

traffic

According to the US Census Bureau, the average American spends 26 minutes communting in a car per day.

Annually, that's about NINE whole days spent behind the wheel, which is certainly not ideal.

environment

Everybody knows that cars release the greenhouse gas carbon dioxide, which is a significant contributor to climate change.

But the amount is somewhat startling: 10,000 pounds per year on average.

Aside from carbon dioxide, cars also release carbon monoxide, sulphur oxide, nitrogen oxide, and lead into the atmosphere.

All of these compounds have their own negative health implications such as breathing and heart problems as well as increased risk of cancer and birth defects.

Once these dangerous chemicals are released, it's extremely difficult to remove them.

It doesn't just affect the driver, it affects us all.

health

Consider ditching your car if you experience any of the following symptoms that are associated with commuting in a car

high blood sugar high cholesterol depression anxiety blood pressure spikes poor sleep decreased cardiovascular fitness back pain or sudden death

money

First you have to actually buy a car. Depending on your preferences, you could spend as little as a couple thousand dollars for a used car (not too bad, huh?) or you could go the new car route and spend an average of $36,000!

Even after you buy the car, you're looking at about $9,000 per year to own it. This includes insurance, gas, maintenance, as well as some other costs. Not good.

if driving is so bad, then what's the alternative?

good: public transport

Taking advantage of the public transportation options available in your area, such as busses, subways, or trains, is an easy way to drive less.

Once you're going, public transport can often get you where you need to go either just as quick or even quicker than driving.

While these public transportation options are great, they still produce some harmful emissions and it can be a big inconvienence when service delays inevitably occur.

better: walk or run

This is perhaps the easiest way to drive less. While walking can take a while depending on how far you need to go, you don't need any additional equipment to walk and you can easily adapt to any changes in the weather

Additionally, you don't need to worry about parking or traffic!

And if you need to get there fast, just start running!

best: bike

Riding a bike is the most efficient method of human powered transportation. Cities often have dedicated bike lanes or bike paths that can get you anywhere you need to go quickly and safely. While you do need to buy and maintain the bike, it is merely a fraction of the cost of buying and maintaining a car.

the bottom line

Overall, any steps you can take to lower your time behind the wheel will result in a happier, healthier lifestyle that is good for you and good for the environment!

good luck!
